What the Data Reveals

The following interactive visualizations highlight key insights from the final project analysis, demonstrating how weather conditions and time-of-day influence bike share usage in Toronto. Each figure provides a unique perspective: from the hourly distribution of ridership to evaluating the accuracy and feature importance of predictive models. These interactive tools allow you to explore patterns and relationships within the data, offering intuitive insights into factors that shape urban bike-sharing behaviors.

Figure 1. An interactive boxplot showing hourly bike trip distribution. Usage peaks around 8 AM and 5 PM, with lower volumes overnight.

Bike usage demonstrates significant variability across the day, peaking notably during commuting hours (8 AM and 5 PM). Early morning (12 AM - 6 AM) and late-night (after 9 PM) hours exhibit lower and more stable ridership, indicating predictable usage patterns. You can hover over each box to see specific statistical details for each hour.
